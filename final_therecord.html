Chapter One · Fall 2025

The Streak Continues

Before the trophies of winter and spring, there was cross country. Morehouse won its 4th consecutive SIAC Cross Country title, a streak stretching back to 2022, led by senior Brian Kemei's third straight SIAC Runner of the Year award and an individual conference title.

It was the opening move in a season no Morehouse team had ever played before: four SIAC championships, one program, one year.

Chapter Two · Winter 2026
SIAC Championship · morehouseathletics.com

Twenty-Three Years

That is how long Morehouse had waited to hold a SIAC Basketball Tournament trophy again. On 8 March 2026, in Coach Larry Dixon's first season, the Maroon Tigers beat Tuskegee 66–56 for the program's 7th SIAC title and a first NCAA Division II Tournament berth since 2018.

“This year, man, we had one of the most historic seasons in school history, if not conference history.”Harold Ellis · Director of Athletics

The 22 wins were a program record for a single season, and back-to-back SIAC Indoor Track titles — the second the inaugural crown the year before — kept the winter just as busy off the hardwood.

Chapter Three · Spring 2026

No. 6, Retired

Four days after recording his 200th career hit, senior shortstop Elijah Pinckney watched his number go up for good. On 18 April 2026, Morehouse retired jersey No. 6 — only the second baseball player and third student-athlete in program history to receive the honor, joining Harold Ellis's own No. 30.

Pinckney closes his career a two-time MBP HBCU All-Star, the first Maroon Tiger picked twice, and an SIAC Elite 16 Academic Honoree carrying a 3.89 GPA. The baseball program he leaves behind finished 25–21, a program record for wins, and reached the SIAC Tournament's final day for the first time since 2005.

The Country Was Watching

Before the trophies, 0 fans packed Oakland Arena for the NBA HBCU Classic during NBA All-Star Weekend — the largest crowd ever to see a Morehouse College team play, on national television. The championship season that followed was ranked No. 22 on BCSN SportsWrap's Top 25 HBCU sports stories of the year.

Off the court, Director of Athletics Harold Ellis was named the inaugural Top Mid-Major Athletic Director by Dr. Cavil's Inside the HBCU Sports Lab — and in the classroom, every program posted a 3.0-plus GPA both semesters, on a combined department GPA of 3.09.
