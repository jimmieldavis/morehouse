Track & Field

The Triple Crown

Cross Country, Indoor Track, and Outdoor Track: all three SIAC titles, same season. No program in SIAC history had done it before.

Victor Chebii swept the 5,000m, 10,000m, and steeplechase at outdoor. Brian Kemei won his third straight SIAC Runner of the Year after taking the individual cross country title. Willie Hill was named SIAC Coach of the Year in both disciplines in the same year.

Baseball · No. 6, Elijah Pinckney

Career: Singular. Season: Special.

On 18 April 2026, Morehouse retired jersey No. 6 for Elijah Pinckney, the second baseball player and third student-athlete in program history, joining Harold Ellis's No. 30.

Four days earlier, Pinckney had recorded his 200th career hit. He closes his Morehouse career a two-time MBP HBCU All-Star, the first Maroon Tiger selected twice, and an SIAC Elite 16 Academic Honoree at a 3.89 GPA.

In 2025, he became the first NCAA Division II player ever to win the T-Mobile Impact Award at the HBCU Swingman Classic, and the first non-Division I player ever selected to play in it.
